Languages of India

North India

North Indian languages are steeped in history and literature, forming the backbone of many cultural traditions in the region.

  • Hindi: Widely spoken in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and Rajasthan.
  • Punjabi: Spoken predominantly in Punjab.

South India

The languages of South India, mainly from the Dravidian family, are known for their rich classical literature and vibrant oral traditions.

  • Tamil: A Dravidian language spoken in Tamil Nadu.
  • Telugu: Spoken in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.

East India

East Indian languages reflect a deep cultural heritage with extensive folklore and literary traditions that span centuries.

  • Odia: The language of Odisha.

West India

Languages of West India mirror the region's diverse cultural interactions, carrying influences from ancient trade routes and regional traditions.

  • Marathi: Spoken in Maharashtra and surrounding areas.

North-East India

North-East India is home to a mosaic of indigenous languages that echo the cultural and tribal diversity of the region.

  • Assamese: The language of Assam.
