Traditional Architecture of India

North India

  • Indo-Saracenic Architecture: A fusion of Hindu, Mughal, and Gothic styles, seen in Delhi’s buildings.
  • Rajasthani Architecture: Characterized by intricate carvings and stunning havelis.

South India

  • Dravidian Architecture: Seen in the temples of Tamil Nadu, featuring towering spires and detailed carvings.
  • Kerala Architecture: Known for sloping roofs and wooden structures, especially in traditional homes and temples.

East India

  • Temple Architecture of Odisha: Famous for temples like the Jagannath Temple at Puri.

West India

  • Maratha Architecture: Found in forts and palaces built by the Maratha Empire.

North-East India

  • Shikhara Style: Seen in the temples of Assam and Meghalaya, with conical roofs.
