Lohri

Lohri is celebrated on 13 January every year, Lohri festival marks the end of peak winter celebrates the harvest of crops. It is a festival to celebrate the harvest of sugarcane crops in January. People light up a bonfire, sing songs, dance and eat winter delicacies made of corn, peanuts, sesame and jaggery. It is a popular festival in Punjab and Jammu.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi, also known as Vaisakhi, is a historical and religious festival in Sikhism and Hinduism. It marks the Sikh New Year and commemorates the formation of Khalsa panth of warriors under Guru Gobind Singh in 1699. It is also a spring harvest festival for the Sikhs.

Hemis

Hemis Festival is a religious festival celebrated at the Hemis Monastery in Ladakh, India. It marks the birth anniversary of Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche), the founder of Tibetan Buddhism. The festival is known for its vibrant masked dances, traditional music, and elaborate costumes.

Shikara Festival

The Shikara Festival is held annually in the Dal Lake of Srinagar, Jammu, and Kashmir. It celebrates the unique culture and heritage of Kashmir. The festival features vibrant shikaras (traditional wooden boats), boat races, and cultural performances.

Gurez Festival

The Gurez Festival is celebrated in the picturesque Gurez Valley in Jammu and Kashmir. It highlights the rich cultural heritage, traditional music, dance, and crafts of the region. The festival aims to promote tourism and showcase the natural beauty of the valley.
