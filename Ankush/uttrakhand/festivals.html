Festivals of Uttarakhand

Kumbh Mela

Kumbh Mela is one of the largest religious gatherings in the world, held every 12 years in Haridwar. It is a significant event for Hindus, where millions of devotees take a holy dip in the Ganges River.

Nanda Devi Raj Jaat

Nanda Devi Raj Jaat is a grand pilgrimage and festival dedicated to Goddess Nanda Devi. It is held once every 12 years and involves a long trek through the Garhwal and Kumaon regions of Uttarakhand.

Basant Panchami

Basant Panchami marks the arrival of spring and is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Uttarakhand. People wear yellow clothes, fly kites, and worship Goddess Saraswati, the deity of knowledge and wisdom.

Phool Dei

Phool Dei is a traditional festival celebrated in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. It marks the beginning of the harvest season, and children go from house to house, offering flowers and receiving sweets in return.

Ghughutiya

Ghughutiya, also known as Makar Sankranti, is celebrated with great fervor in Uttarakhand. People prepare sweets made of jaggery and sesame seeds, and children wear garlands of these sweets and sing traditional songs.

Uttarakhand Culture

Experience the vibrant culture and rich heritage of Uttarakhand. From historical monuments to colorful festivals, Uttarakhand offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity.
