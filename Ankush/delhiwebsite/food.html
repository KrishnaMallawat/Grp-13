Food Culture of Delhi

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that consists of spicy chickpeas (chole) served with deep-fried bread (bhature). It is a favorite breakfast item in Delhi and is enjoyed by people of all ages.

Paratha

Paratha is a type of flatbread that is commonly eaten for breakfast in Delhi. It can be stuffed with various fillings such as potatoes, paneer, or cauliflower, and is usually served with yogurt, pickles, and butter.

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a rich and creamy dish made with marinated chicken cooked in a tomato-based gravy. It is a staple in Delhi's cuisine and is best enjoyed with naan or rice.

Chaat

Chaat is a savory snack that is popular in Delhi's street food culture. It includes a variety of ingredients such as potatoes, chickpeas, yogurt, tamarind chutney, and spices, creating a burst of flavors in every bite.

Kulfi

Kulfi is a traditional Indian ice cream that is denser and creamier than regular ice cream. It comes in various flavors such as mango, pistachio, and saffron, and is a popular dessert in Delhi, especially during the summer.

Nihari

Nihari is a slow-cooked stew consisting of meat, mainly shank meat of beef or lamb, along with bone marrow. It is a popular dish in Delhi, especially enjoyed as a breakfast item.

Daulat Ki Chaat

Daulat Ki Chaat is a unique dessert made from milk froth, saffron, and sugar. It is a winter delicacy in Delhi and is known for its light and airy texture.

Bedmi Puri

Bedmi Puri is a popular breakfast dish in Delhi, consisting of deep-fried puris made from wheat flour and stuffed with a spicy mixture of urad dal. It is usually served with aloo sabzi (potato curry).

Ram Ladoo

Ram Ladoo is a popular street food snack in Delhi, made from fried lentil fritters topped with grated radish and served with tangy green chutney.
