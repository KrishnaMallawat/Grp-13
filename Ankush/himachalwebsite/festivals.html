Festivals of Himachal Pradesh

Kullu Dussehra

Kullu Dussehra is a renowned festival celebrated in the Kullu Valley. It marks the victory of good over evil and is celebrated with great enthusiasm. The festival includes processions, cultural performances, and traditional rituals.

Losar Festival

Losar is the Tibetan New Year festival celebrated in the Lahaul-Spiti region of Himachal Pradesh. It is marked by traditional dances, music, and rituals to welcome the new year and bid farewell to the old one.

Maha Shivratri

Maha Shivratri is a significant Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is celebrated with great devotion in Himachal Pradesh, especially in the Mandi district, where a grand fair is held.

Minjar Festival

Minjar Festival is celebrated in the Chamba district to mark the harvest season. It includes cultural performances, sports events, and a grand procession. The festival is named after the maize flower, which symbolizes prosperity.

Halda Festival

Halda Festival is celebrated in the Lahaul region to honor the goddess of wealth, Shashikar Apa. It involves lighting bonfires, traditional dances, and rituals to seek blessings for prosperity and well-being.

Himachal Pradesh Culture

Experience the vibrant culture and rich heritage of Himachal Pradesh. From historical monuments to colorful festivals, Himachal Pradesh offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity.
