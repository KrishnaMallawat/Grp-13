Indian States and Their Textile History

Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh is known for its Kalamkari textiles, which involve intricate hand-painted or block-printed designs on fabric. The state is also famous for Pochampally ikat weaving.

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is renowned for its traditional handlooms and weaves, including shawls and skirts with tribal patterns and vibrant colors.

Assam

Assam is famous for its silk textiles, especially Muga silk, known for its natural golden sheen. Eri and Pat silks are also highly regarded.

Bihar

Bihar is known for its Madhubani painting textiles and Bhagalpuri silk, often referred to as the "Queen of Silks."

Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is renowned for Kosa silk, produced in tribal regions and known for its unique texture and natural dyes.

Goa

Goa is known for its Kunbi sarees, a traditional weave that reflects the state's rich cultural heritage.

Gujarat

Gujarat is famous for its Bandhani (tie-dye) textiles, Patola silk sarees, and intricate embroidery work like Zari and mirror work.

Haryana

Haryana is known for its handloom products, including durries (rugs) and shawls, often featuring geometric patterns.

Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is renowned for its woolen shawls, particularly those from Kullu, known for their vibrant patterns and fine quality.

Jammu and Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir is known for it's rich Pashmina Shawls

Jharkhand

Jharkhand is famous for its Tussar silk, produced by tribal communities using traditional methods.

Karnataka

Karnataka is known for its Mysore silk sarees, which are characterized by their rich colors and intricate gold zari work.

Kerala

Kerala is famous for its Kasavu sarees, which are handwoven with gold borders and often worn during festive occasions.

Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh is renowned for its Chanderi and Maheshwari sarees, which are known for their lightweight texture and intricate zari work.

Maharashtra

Maharashtra is famous for its Paithani sarees, characterized by vibrant colors and intricate peacock motifs in zari work.

Manipur

Manipur is known for its handwoven textiles, particularly the Phanek and the intricate designs of Manipuri silk sarees.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya is renowned for its handwoven shawls and textiles made from natural fibers like cotton and eri silk.

Mizoram

Mizoram is famous for its traditional puan, a handwoven wrap-around garment with vibrant colors and intricate designs.

Nagaland

Nagaland is known for its vibrant tribal shawls, each design symbolizing the wearer's social status and tribe.

Odisha

Odisha is famous for its ikat weaves, known locally as "Bandha," and the intricate applique work of Pipili.

Punjab

Punjab is renowned for its Phulkari embroidery, characterized by vibrant floral patterns on shawls and dupattas.

Rajasthan

Rajasthan is known for its Bandhani (tie-dye) textiles, block prints, and the luxurious Kota Doria sarees.

Sikkim

Sikkim is known for its traditional Lepcha weaves, often crafted into shawls and other garments with intricate patterns.

Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu is famous for its Kanchipuram silk sarees, known for their durability, vibrant colors, and rich zari work.

Telangana

Telangana is renowned for its Pochampally ikat weaving, which produces unique geometric patterns on sarees and fabrics.

Tripura

Tripura is known for its handwoven tribal fabrics, including Risa, Riha, and Rikutu shawls, crafted with traditional motifs.

Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh is famous for its Banarasi silk sarees, characterized by intricate brocade work with gold and silver threads.

Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is known for its woolen shawls and garments made from natural fibers, often featuring traditional motifs.

West Bengal

West Bengal is famous for its Baluchari sarees, which depict mythological scenes, and the fine muslin of Bengal.
